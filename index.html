Experience

As a dual major in Biology and Computer Science, I've accumulated a wide range of experiences in both fields which I ultimately hope to synthesize together one day.

Research Intern at US Food and Drug Administration (Summer 2018)

  • Created and presented two models on how artificial sweeteners' chemical structure damages human gut flora and leads to environmental bioaccumulation.

    • Research Intern at Radify Labs (Summer 2019)

  • Employed Numpy and Pandas for heart disease patient data analysis and Tableau to visualized patterns in patient data
  • Constructed random forest and decision tree diagnostic models using Tensorflow. These diagnostic models were built from the UCI Cleveland heart disease dataset, and were able to reach 85% diagnosis accuracy.

    • Team Leader at MIT GrandHack DC (August 2019)

  • Developed Breathe Easy, a prototype for a continuous remote asthma monitoring device which could decrease preventable ER visits for asthma patients by up to 65%, saving patients and insurance companies millions.
  • Won Runner-up in the Access to Healthcare track, and currently pursuing patent paperwork.

    • Developer for Machine Learning 4 Virginia (Spring 2020)

  • As a final ML4VA project, I developed a CNN-based automatic recyclables sorter which reached 90% accuracy and could help Virginia reach its environmental goals

    • Open Source ML Researcher at NASA Frontier Development Labs (Summer 2020)

  • Developed a variety of sequence-to-sequence LSTM and CNN models for NASA's Frontier Development Lab under mentorship from Anirudh Koul and Siddha Ganju. These models ranged from a LSTM meteor shower classifier to a multimodal CNN-based pedestrian trajectory predictor.
